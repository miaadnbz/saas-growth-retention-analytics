# Executive Brief: SaaS Growth and Retention

## Decision required

Choose the next-quarter growth plan that improves retained subscription revenue rather than maximizing trial volume.

## What the data says

- Overall activation: **51.9%** across 12,000 synthetic trials.
- Overall trial-to-paid conversion: **46.8%**.
- Activated paid accounts reached **86.4% 90-day retention**, compared with **65.2%** for non-activated paid accounts.
- Referral produced the strongest observed revenue efficiency at **6.86x revenue-to-spend**.
- Paid Social activation was **50.8%**, leaving the largest practical onboarding opportunity among high-volume paid acquisition sources.

## Recommendation

1. Launch a four-week onboarding experiment for Paid Social and Paid Search trials that have not completed two critical actions by day 3.
2. Keep 10% of the eligible population as a randomized holdout.
3. Use 14-day activation as the primary metric; trial conversion, 90-day retention, support tickets, and unsubscribe rate as downstream or guardrail metrics.
4. Reallocate budget only after incremental retained CAC is measured—not from attributed conversions alone.

## Quantified opportunity

A 10-percentage-point activation improvement among Paid Social trials is associated with an estimated **81 additional 90-day retained paid accounts** and **$95,945 annualized recurring revenue** in this synthetic scenario.

This is an assumption-based planning estimate. The experiment is intended to replace that assumption with a causal estimate.

## Executive message

> The growth constraint is not trial volume; it is the share of acquired accounts that reach collaborative value quickly. Marketing should optimize toward retained CAC, while Product should make teammate invitation and integration connection visible onboarding outcomes.
